# Probing Nehaï et al.'s TLA+ swap proof — sibling work, modeling idioms, and adversarial handles

Nehaï et al. (ICDCN 2022) stand alone as the **only mechanized, Byzantine-aware TLA+/TLAPS proof of a cross-chain swap to date**, but the proof rests on three load-bearing abstractions — a *correct* smart-contract coordinator, *unforgeable* proofs-of-action, and a Byzantine-as-either-branch modeling idiom — that each have well-mapped failure surfaces in the literature. The paper's "Consistency" property is Herlihy-2018's "no conforming party worse-off" under a different name; it is strictly weaker than Zakhary's AC3WN "all-or-nothing" and strictly weaker than van Glabbeek's "success guarantee". The $n \leq 6$ model-checking ceiling is structural for concrete-process TLC and cannot be closed by incremental tuning. Post-2022 citation traffic is thin (≈8 works as of April 2026): no follow-up has audited the TLAPS proof itself, none has completed the "abstract-to-instantiated" refinement the authors flag as future work, and the closest methodological competitor (Llambías et al., Event-B/Rodin at ICBC 2024) targets gateway-style interoperability and does not speak to the TLA+ proof. A claims-first reimplementation therefore has three fruitful lines of attack: reopen the coordinator as a Byzantine entity, expose the proof-of-action abstraction to a validator-set model with $f \geq n/3$ corruption, and probe the pc-indexed invariant for the "silent Byzantine" envelope.

## Where Nehaï sits in the cross-chain FV landscape

The seven canonical cross-chain-swap formalization papers partition cleanly on **three axes**: who the coordinator is, what adversary they tolerate, and what "atomicity" means to them.

**Atomicity is the fault line.** Five inequivalent definitions coexist in these papers, and glossing between them is precisely where reimplementation bugs hide.

Zakhary et al. claim **decentralized all-or-nothing**: "either all smart contracts in an AC2T are redeemed or all of them are refunded" (AC3WN §4.2, Lemma 5.1). Nehaï rejects this framing as structurally unreachable under Byzantine: "*since the system tolerates Byzantine participants, the classical atomicity definition 'all-or-nothing' cannot be applied … It is impossible to force a participant to initiate the transfer of its asset*" (ICDCN §4). **This is not a difference in strength so much as a difference in scope**: AC3WN implicitly assumes every participant reaches the lock phase (its protocol graph "starts" only after all locks are deployed; a source that never locks is outside the protocol), which silently crash-quotients the case Nehaï insists on modeling, where a Byzantine source *refuses* to lock. Under that observation the two definitions are close to equivalent on the intersection of their domains; Nehaï's weakening simply makes the uncovered domain explicit.

Herlihy–Liskov–Shrira (VLDB'19) anticipate this: they explicitly critique Herlihy-2018 for being too narrow ("*Herlihy assumed that any swap outcome where a party receives only partial inputs and partial outputs is unacceptable, but the notions of correctness introduced here allow parties to specify whether some such partial deal outcomes are acceptable*"), which is a different generalization axis (per-party preferences) than Nehaï's Byzantine-weakening. Nehaï's Consistency is a *specialization* of HLS-2019's safety to the all-or-none preference, and a *renaming* of Herlihy-2018's "no conforming party worse-off". **This is not a new correctness notion; it is Herlihy-2018 mechanized.**

van Glabbeek et al.'s "success guarantee" is strictly stronger than both and proven impossible in asynchrony — a result Nehaï's paper does not engage with and which implicitly constrains their *Retrieving* property: Retrieving requires synchrony precisely because the van Glabbeek impossibility bites.

Hirai's Kripke-model analysis is the oddity worth internalizing for a reimplementation: he shows atomicity of the Nolan/Herlihy HTLC *fails without strong temporal-epistemic premises*. Nehaï's smart-contract coordinator is effectively an engineering device for discharging those premises (the coordinator's trace is common knowledge by blockchain publication), which should be made explicit in the invariant.

**Post-2022 citations carry no technical audit of the proof.** Semantic Scholar and ACM DL list ~8 citations of Nehaï 2022 as of April 2026. The substantive ones:

- **Llambías, González, Ruggia (ICBC 2024 and ICBC 2025)** argue the protocol-agnostic P_swap abstraction does not model gateway-mediated (lock-and-mint) interoperability and propose Event-B/Rodin as an alternative formalism. This is a scope critique, not a correctness audit. Artifact on Zenodo (10.5281/zenodo.10956083).
- **Chan–Chrobak–Lesani, "Cross-chain Swaps with Preferences" (CSF 2023, arXiv:2210.11791)** attacks the fixed-graph/fixed-preference assumption common to Herlihy-2018 and Nehaï, proves deciding existence of a safe/live/SNE protocol is NP-hard / Σ₂ᴾ-complete under preference lattices. Implicit critique: the Nehaï problem statement is too coarse for any realistic market.
- **Pillai, Hóu, Biswas, Muthukkumarasamy, "Formal Verification of the Burn-to-Claim Protocol" (ICFEM 2023)** is the nearest *technical* sibling: they model a coordinator-free lock-and-burn cross-chain transfer in PAT (Process Analysis Toolkit, CSP#+LTL) rather than TLA+, verifying at the network level with explicit miner/user processes. Artifact at github.com/b-pillai/Burn-to-Claim-formal-verification. They explicitly do not handle Byzantine validators.
- No paper locates a bug in Nehaï's TLAPS proof, and no paper has completed the abstract-to-instantiated refinement the authors flag as future work.

## TLA+ modeling patterns that Nehaï instantiates — and their ceilings

Nehaï's model combines two canonical idioms that the distributed-systems TLA+ community has converged on. **Byzantine-as-nondeterministic-either-branch** wraps each honest step in `Correct(p) \/ Byz(p)`, where `Byz(p)` non-deterministically mutates local state within an envelope constrained by cryptographic axioms on signatures. **Message-set abstraction** treats the inter-process medium as a monotone set, with the adversary able to append any well-typed message whose authenticated fields they can produce. The reference instances are Lamport's *Byzantizing Paxos by Refinement* (DISC 2011), where 3f+1 acceptors include arbitrary-message senders bounded only by unforgeability, and the Informal Systems Tendermint spec (`TendermintAcc_004_draft.tla`), where disjoint `CONSTANTS Corr, Faulty` partition the node set and a `FaultyBroadcast` action injects messages constrained by `|Faulty| ≤ T`.

**The tractability win of the either-branch pattern is also its completeness risk.** Inductive invariants can be structured as `Corr ⇒ Φ(p, pc(p))` — the invariant says nothing about Byzantine local state — which is exactly Nehaï's `CoordInv2`, organized as a conjunction of per-pc-label predicates (`CoordInv2_INIT ∧ _PUBLISHED ∧ _WAITFORD ∧ _OK ∧ _RF ∧ _ABORT`). The dual risk: the envelope `Byz(p)` may underapproximate "silent Byzantine" (a participant that does nothing but also doesn't take the Byzantine action) or "stalling with stale messages". If so, liveness can be vacuously established by any trace in which the adversary obligingly takes observable actions. The Nehaï paper narrows the envelope further with "proof-of-action cannot be forged", which *assumption-closes* the gap between the Byzantine transition and the cryptographic reality — a modeling move that is convenient for proofs but shifts the burden to validating the assumption.

The **per-pc-label invariant conjunction pattern** has no single canonical name in the TLA+ community. Floyd (1967) called them location assertions; Chand–Liu–Stoller (FM 2016, arXiv:1606.01387) call them *process invariants* (as distinct from *message invariants* in Paxos-family specs); Schultz, Dardik, Tripakis (CPP 2022, MongoRaftReconfig) group them as L1/L2/C1/C2. Pragmatically, Nehaï's CoordInv2 is a standard instance of what the community treats as the *default* inductive-invariant shape for protocol-level TLAPS proofs. **The specific risk for reimplementation**: a pc-indexed invariant conjunction is easy to write but hard to verify *complete* — it is common to strengthen one conjunct enough to discharge an OBVIOUS at the cost of a vacuous clause elsewhere.

**The n=6 ceiling is structural, not engineering.** Nehaï's TLC table stops at 6 participants; the all-Byzantine case OOMs. This matches the community median almost exactly: Informal Systems reports n=4..6 for Tendermint-Apalache; Kukharenko's HotStuff TLA+ (NID 2020) stops near n=4 in TLC and n=6..10 in Apalache; Konnov's recent Apalache Ben-Or check at n=6,t=1,f=1 took "≈1 day 20 hours" (protocols-made-fun.com, 2024). The three escape routes from this ceiling are (i) TLAPS-verified *refinement* onto a correct-only protocol à la Lamport 2011, which is parametric in n and requires no TLC, (ii) Apalache's `--inductive` mode on a manually strengthened CoordInv2, (iii) porting to threshold automata via ByMC (Konnov–Lazić–Stoilkovska–Widder survey, LMCS 2023, arXiv:2011.14789) — but the cross-chain swap is *not* a threshold-guarded protocol in the Bracha/Bosco sense, so (iii) is mostly unavailable. **Nehaï uses none of these.** The paper reports only TLAPS for safety and TLC for liveness; this is why the liveness claim is small-model rather than parametric, and why an adversarial reimplementation that scales to n≥8 with a validator subset would be a publishable delta.

For reference patterns to imitate when rebuilding:

- **Lamport, "Byzantizing Paxos by Refinement" (DISC 2011)** — the refinement blueprint; derive the Byzantine swap as a refinement of a correct-only swap where Byzantine actions refine stuttering, with auxiliary variables for proof-of-action tokens (Lamport–Merz arXiv:1703.05121 on history/prophecy is the accompanying methodology paper).
- **Tendermint's `TendermintAcc_004_draft.tla` + Apalache-checked inductive invariant** — the closest living cousin of Nehaï's style, and the canonical answer to "what does a 2020s TLA+ Byzantine spec look like?".
- **Chand–Liu–Stoller Multi-Paxos TLAPS (FM 2016)** — the methodology for decomposing an inductive proof into per-action × per-conjunct obligations without running TLAPS out of memory.
- **Braithwaite–Buchman–Khoffi–Konnov–Milosevic–Ruetschi–Widder, "A Tendermint Light Client" (arXiv:2010.07031)** — the only TLA+ spec of cross-chain-relevant verification that explicitly models a *dynamic validator set* with bounded trust period, checked with Apalache. This is the spec to study for how to ground Nehaï's proof-of-action abstraction in a realistic f<n/3 committee model.

## Five vectors where the paper's abstractions push risk outside the proof

**Coordinator correctness is the largest risk transfer.** Nehaï: "*In a blockchain context, the coordinator's logic is implemented as a smart contract. This implementation allows considering the coordinator [as correct]*." and "*Crucially, the proof-of-action and the coordinator correctness restrict the Byzantine behaviour in a predetermined and detectable way. Consequently, it helps us carry out the formal proof of the protocol so that the presence of Byzantine failures does not add significant complexity.*" (ICDCN §3.2.1). The TLA+ model treats the coordinator as a single correct process with persistent state; **it does not model the consensus that backs the coordinator's host chain**. Under a chain reorg on the host chain, the coordinator's `loc(c)` and any intermediate decisions (`OK`, `RF`, `ABORT`) can be rolled back; the Nehaï `TypeOk` + `CoordInv2` do not have an action that decreases `loc(c)` toward an earlier label, so a reorg that uncommits a decision already observed by a recipient violates `CoordInv2` by construction — or, worse, passes the TLAPS check because the model simply cannot express it. This is a textbook Hirai-style failure: the atomicity theorem holds only under the epistemic premise that the coordinator's ledger is final. Under MEV or censorship on the host chain, the *Retrieving* liveness (which already requires synchrony) degrades further: a colluding miner set can deterministically delay the coordinator's decision past any fixed timeout. Zakhary's AC3WN makes this risk explicit (they assume "no forks in the witness network" as a precondition of Lemma 5.1); Nehaï does not state the analogous precondition.

**The proof-of-action abstraction hides validator corruption.** Nehaï: "*A proof-of-action, once provided, cannot be forged, even if a Byzantine participant provides the proof*" and "*a Byzantine participant could try to double-spend an asset already locked in its blockchain. However, in the case where the asset is no longer in the hands of the Byzantine, the proof-of-action will not be valid, leading to the abortion of the transfer*" (§3). In a BFT-backed chain this unforgeability holds only while validator corruption on the source chain stays below the consensus threshold (typically $f < n/3$ via $2f + 1$ BLS signature aggregation). Under $f \geq n/3$ on any one of the swapped chains, an adversary can produce a proof-of-action for a double-spent asset; Nehaï's Consistency theorem fails at the abstraction boundary, not inside the TLAPS proof. The relevant reference is the **Braithwaite–Buchman–Khoffi–Konnov–Milosevic–Ruetschi–Widder Tendermint Light Client** (arXiv:2010.07031), which explicitly formalizes a dynamic validator set with trusting-period-bounded correctness and Apalache-checked attack-detection invariants. Their fault model is the correct one for Nehaï's instantiated protocol, but Nehaï does not refine onto it. **Conformity between abstract P_swap and instantiated P_inst is the paper's own-stated future work**, and it is exactly this gap. Pillai et al.'s Burn-to-Claim PAT/CSP model (ICFEM 2023) has the opposite problem: it models the network-layer miners concretely but punts on cryptographic abstractions.

**Weakened atomicity is rhetorical, not substantive, but the *scope* is.** As analyzed above, Nehaï's Consistency equals Herlihy-2018's "no conforming party worse off"; the "weakening" over Zakhary is largely an artifact of domain scope (Byzantine source that refuses to lock). Where the paper *does* diverge is in what it quantifies over: Consistency is established only for "correct participant"; a Byzantine participant that *is* the recipient and that *never attempts* the retrieve action is simply outside the property's universe. This is defensible (cf. Herlihy–Liskov–Shrira's Theorem 2 "*no compliant party's outgoing assets are locked up forever*") but produces an easy false-sense-of-safety: the paper's weak-liveness Ownership does *not* guarantee that assets in the coordinator's escrow reach a final state; it only guarantees that a correct recipient who calls retrieve eventually gets a decision.

**The TLC model is small and the TLAPS proof is not audited.** Liveness (Ownership, Retrieving) is checked only with TLC at up to 6 participants, and the paper reports the all-Byzantine case exhausted memory. There is no cutoff theorem justifying n=6 — the justification is purely pragmatic. As of April 2026 no follow-up paper has completed the abstract-to-instantiated refinement the authors flag as future work. No paper has located a specific gap in the TLAPS proof, which is consistent with but does not establish its correctness.

**Coordinator-free variants exist but are under-formalized.** Herlihy's 2018 HTLC protocol is coordinator-free by design; no TLA+/TLAPS proof of it under Byzantine participants has been published. Imoto et al. 2019 (SSS) is pen-paper under rational deviation. Pillai et al.'s Burn-to-Claim is the only mechanized cross-chain-transfer FV that removes a coordinator, but it is in CSP/LTL and does not model Byzantine validators. **This is an open gap.** The Tendermint IBC specification occupies the middle ground — it has a TLA+ spec and a live validator model — but is not a swap protocol.

## Five adversarial probes for the reimplementation

The following are concrete model variants and invariants to run against an independent TLA+ encoding of Consistency, Ownership, Retrieving. Each maps to a risk surface above.

**1. Reorg-capable coordinator.** Add a `CoordRollback(k)` action that, given `k ≥ 1` and the coordinator's current pc = L, sets `loc(c)` back to the pc at step `depth(L) − k` and discards any `actionLog` entries emitted after that depth; guard the action by `ReorgEnabled = TRUE` as a parameter. Assert CoordInv2 against this extended Next. The expected finding is that at least one of the six per-pc conjuncts — almost certainly CoordInv2_OK or CoordInv2_RF — fails because a recipient's observation of an `OK` decision can be retroactively invalidated. To recover, the invariant must be strengthened with a finality predicate over `loc(c)`, or the model restricted to a parameter `ReorgDepth = 0`. The probe quantifies exactly how much host-chain finality is required for Consistency to hold.

**2. Silent-Byzantine envelope completeness.** Add a `ByzStutter(p)` action for `p \in Faulty` that has enabling guard `TRUE` and updates `UNCHANGED vars`. Re-check Ownership (weak liveness: eventually a correct recipient with a valid proof-of-action reaches a decision). If the original spec's `Byz(p)` envelope already subsumed stuttering, this is a no-op; if it did not, Ownership will now fail because a colluding Byzantine subset can freeze progress by never emitting proofs while never taking an overtly Byzantine action. The probe surfaces whether the paper's liveness is robust to the hardest Byzantine strategy (do nothing).

**3. Validator-corruption refinement for proof-of-action.** Replace the opaque `ProofOfAction(a)` predicate with a concrete `CONSTANT Validators`, `Faulty ⊆ Validators`, `|Faulty| = f`, `|Validators| = n`, and `ProofOfAction(a) == ∃ S ⊆ Validators: |S| ≥ n − f ∧ a ∈ Signed(v) ∀ v ∈ S`. Parameterize over (n, f) and run TLC/Apalache for (n, f) ∈ {(4, 1), (7, 2), (3, 1), (3, 2)}. Expected: Consistency holds for f < n/3 (the committee threshold) and fails at f ≥ n/3 because a corrupt committee can sign a proof-of-action for an asset the participant has double-spent. The probe operationalizes the conformity proof the authors defer, and gives an exact value of f at which Consistency breaks — which is the real Byzantine bound the paper implicitly assumes.

**4. Byzantine-coordinator variant.** Rewrite the coordinator as an ordinary participant process, with `c ∈ Faulty` possible. Retain `proof-of-action` unforgeability but drop "coordinator correctness". Re-run Consistency. Expected: the property fails; the counterexample trace will be illuminating — it typically shows a Byzantine coordinator emitting `OK` to one recipient and `ABORT` to another, which is the scenario AC3WN's witness-chain replication is designed to prevent. To rescue Consistency one can either reintroduce a quorum witness (Zakhary path) or adopt Herlihy-2018's coordinator-free HTLC structure. The probe directly quantifies what the "coordinator correct" assumption buys, and produces a falsifiable comparison against AC3WN.

**5. CoordInv2 inductiveness under CTI mining with Apalache.** Port the spec to Apalache and run `apalache-mc check --inductive` against CoordInv2 as stated in the paper. Mine counterexamples-to-induction by weakening one conjunct at a time; for each CTI, classify as (a) vacuous (the weakened conjunct was never load-bearing), (b) fixable by a small strengthening, or (c) symptomatic of a genuine gap in the published TLAPS proof. This is the most likely place to find an actual soundness issue: per-pc invariant conjunctions of this shape routinely include one conjunct that survives TLAPS's OBVIOUS only because a sibling conjunct silently carries it, a flaw invisible to pen-and-paper review and to TLC but detectable by Apalache's SMT back end.

## What a rigorous delta looks like

Nehaï et al. deliver the first mechanized TLA+/TLAPS safety proof of a cross-chain swap, but they do so by assuming away the three hardest parts of the problem: coordinator fault-tolerance, validator corruption, and parametric scaling. A claims-first reimplementation that (i) refines the abstract coordinator onto a finite-fork or Byzantine-coordinator model, (ii) refines proof-of-action onto a concrete (n, f) committee signature scheme, and (iii) uses Apalache's inductive mode to escape the n=6 ceiling, would close exactly the gaps the paper itself identifies as future work, and would for the first time situate a cross-chain swap proof alongside the mature Paxos/Tendermint BFT verification line rather than beside it. The five probes above are the scaffolding: each one either confirms an assumption the paper makes *explicit* (and so validates the proof), or exposes an assumption the paper makes *implicit* (and so identifies the real frontier).
